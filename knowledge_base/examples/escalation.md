## Context

Use this example when the creator's counter-offer exceeds the max-without-approval ceiling and the deal must be escalated to a team lead or manager for review. The goal is to acknowledge their rate respectfully, signal that you need internal alignment, and buy time without losing momentum or making the creator feel undervalued.

## Email

Hi {name},

Thank you for sharing your rate -- I really appreciate the transparency. Your content quality and audience engagement definitely justify premium pricing, and I want to make sure we put together a package that reflects that.

The rate of {their_rate} is a bit above what I can confirm on my own, so I'd like to loop in my team to see what we can do. I want to be upfront about that rather than go back and forth without being able to commit.

I should have an update for you within {timeframe}. In the meantime, please don't hesitate to reach out if you have any questions about the campaign.

Thanks again for your interest in working together -- I'm optimistic we can make something work.

Best,
{sender_name}

## Key Tactics

- **Empathy:** Validates the creator's rate and acknowledges their value before introducing the constraint -- this prevents the creator from feeling rejected
- **Pause:** Buys time for internal review without creating dead air or uncertainty -- the creator knows to expect a response within a specific timeframe
- **Escalation signal:** Frames the escalation as process ("I need to loop in my team") rather than reluctance ("your rate is too high") -- this preserves the positive negotiation energy
- **Transparency:** Being upfront about authority limits builds trust rather than making empty promises or stalling

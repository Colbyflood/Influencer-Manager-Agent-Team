## Context

Use this example when the creator's rate is significantly above budget and no combination of levers (reduced deliverables, shorter usage rights, product offers) can bridge the gap. The goal is to decline gracefully while preserving the relationship for future campaigns.

## Email

Hi {name},

Thank you so much for taking the time to discuss this campaign with us. We've really enjoyed learning more about your content and audience -- your work is genuinely impressive.

After reviewing the numbers on our end, we're not able to make the budget work for this particular campaign. Your rates absolutely reflect the quality of your content and the audience you've built, and we respect that completely.

We'd love to keep you on our radar for future campaigns where we might have more flexibility. If you're open to it, we'll reach out when something comes up that could be a better fit.

Thanks again, and wishing you all the best with your upcoming projects!

Best,
{sender_name}

## Key Tactics

- **Gratitude:** Opens and closes with genuine appreciation -- the creator should feel valued even though the deal didn't work out
- **Relationship preservation:** Explicitly acknowledges that the creator's rates are fair and reasonable -- the issue is budget fit, not creator worth
- **Future door:** Leaves the relationship open for future campaigns without making hollow promises -- "if you're open to it" gives the creator agency
- **Brevity:** Keeps the decline to three short paragraphs. Over-explaining can feel patronizing or insincere

## Context

Use this example when the cash rate is at or near the max-without-approval ceiling but the gap to the creator's ask is small (within 15-20%). Adding product value can bridge the gap without exceeding cash budget. Always position product as additive compensation, never as a substitute for fair cash payment.

## Email

Hi {name},

I appreciate your flexibility as we work through the details. We're really close to making this work, and I want to put something together that feels right for both sides.

Here's an updated proposal:

- **Deliverables:** {deliverables}
- **Cash Rate:** {cash_rate}
- **Usage Rights:** {usage_rights_duration}
- **Plus:** We'd love to include {product_description} (valued at {product_value}) as part of the partnership package

The total value of the partnership comes to {total_value}, which we think reflects the quality of content you produce and the reach of your audience. The {product_description} is something we think you'll genuinely enjoy and that could also make for great content if you're interested in incorporating it.

Let me know if this package works for you! We're excited about this collaboration and want to make sure it feels like a win on your end too.

Best,
{sender_name}

## Key Tactics

- **Product leverage:** Bridges the gap between cash ceiling and creator's ask using tangible product value -- this keeps total compensation competitive without exceeding cash budget
- **Added value:** Positions the product as genuinely valuable to the creator (not a consolation prize) by noting its retail value and content potential
- **Creative framing:** Suggests the product could enhance the content itself, making it feel like a creative asset rather than a perk
- **Total value framing:** Presents the combined cash + product value as the "total partnership value" so the creator sees the full picture

## Context

Use this example when the creator's rate is above budget and reducing the usage rights duration is the most effective lever. This works particularly well when the campaign doesn't strictly require long-term usage rights, or when the creator's rate is heavily influenced by usage licensing. Trading from 12-month to 6-month (or 6-month to 3-month) usage can significantly reduce the total rate.

## Email

Hi {name},

Thanks for sharing your rate for the campaign. I can see the value you bring, and I want to find a way to make this work within our budget.

One thing I'd like to explore: would you be open to adjusting the usage rights window? Here's what I'm thinking:

- **Original ask:** {deliverables} with {original_duration} usage rights at {their_rate}
- **Adjusted proposal:** {deliverables} with {reduced_duration} usage rights at {adjusted_rate}

By shortening the usage window from {original_duration} to {reduced_duration}, we can close the gap on rate while still getting the content we need for the campaign's core run. If the campaign performs well, we'd be happy to discuss extending usage rights down the road.

This keeps your per-deliverable rate strong while making the total cost work on our end. Let me know what you think!

Best,
{sender_name}

## Key Tactics

- **Usage rights trading:** Explicitly trades duration for rate reduction -- this is one of the most effective levers because creators (and especially talent managers) understand that shorter usage = lower cost
- **Duration reduction:** Shows both the original and adjusted terms side by side so the creator can clearly see the trade-off and make an informed decision
- **Cost savings:** Frames the trade as mutually beneficial -- the creator maintains their per-deliverable rate integrity while the total cost fits the budget
- **Extension hint:** Mentioning the possibility of extending usage rights later creates goodwill and leaves room for future revenue for the creator

## Context

Use this example when presenting a multi-deliverable package at a bundled rate. This works especially well as an initial offer (anchoring with more deliverables) or as a counter-offer that reframes the conversation around total package value rather than per-deliverable rate. The bundled approach makes the per-unit economics more attractive for both sides.

## Email

Hi {name},

Thanks so much for your interest in partnering with us! We've been following your content and love the way you {specific_content_compliment}.

We'd love to put together a package that gives your audience multiple touchpoints with the brand. Here's what we're thinking:

**Campaign Package:**
- {deliverable_1} (e.g., 1 IG Reel)
- {deliverable_2} (e.g., 3 IG Stories)
- {deliverable_3} (e.g., 1 TikTok Video)
- **Usage Rights:** {usage_rights_duration}
- **Total Rate:** {bundled_rate}

This comes out to roughly {per_deliverable_rate} per deliverable, which we think reflects a fair value for the quality of content you produce. The multi-deliverable format also gives your audience a richer experience with the brand, which tends to perform really well.

Let me know your thoughts! Happy to discuss the package structure if you'd like to adjust anything.

Best,
{sender_name}

## Key Tactics

- **Bundling:** Presents multiple deliverables as a single package with one total rate -- this shifts the conversation from "what's your rate for one Reel" to "here's the total value of the partnership"
- **Value framing:** Breaks down the per-deliverable rate to show the economics are fair, while keeping the total package as the anchor number
- **Deliverable tiers:** The package is structured at Scenario 1 or 2 level, giving room to trade down to fewer deliverables if the creator's counter is higher than expected
- **Flexibility signal:** "Happy to discuss the package structure" signals willingness to adjust, which invites negotiation rather than a binary accept/reject

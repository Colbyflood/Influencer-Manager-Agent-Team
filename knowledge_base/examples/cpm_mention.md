## Context

Use this example when negotiating with a data-driven creator or talent manager who responds well to metrics-based conversations. Share CPM data to justify your rate position transparently. Only use this approach when the creator seems analytically minded and the rate gap is bridgeable -- sharing CPM data won't help when the gap is 3x or more.

## Email

Hi {name},

Thanks for coming back with your thoughts on the rate. I want to be transparent about how we approach pricing so we can find the right number together.

For this type of campaign, we typically target a CPM of around {cpm_target}. Based on your average of {average_views} views per {content_type}, that puts us at a rate of approximately {calculated_rate} for the deliverables we discussed.

Your counter of {their_rate} is above that range, but I understand your content consistently delivers strong engagement. Here's what I think we can do to get closer:

- We could adjust the deliverable package to {adjusted_deliverables} at {adjusted_rate}
- Alternatively, if we shorten usage rights to {shorter_duration}, we could offer {usage_adjusted_rate}

I'm sharing these numbers because I want you to see the logic behind our pricing -- not to undervalue your work. Your content is exactly what we're looking for, and I'd love to find a structure that works for both of us.

Let me know what resonates!

Best,
{sender_name}

## Key Tactics

- **Data-backed:** Grounds the rate proposal in measurable metrics (CPM, average views) rather than subjective assessments -- this feels fair and objective
- **CPM justification:** Shows the creator exactly how the rate was calculated, which builds credibility and makes the number feel less arbitrary
- **Transparency:** Framing the CPM share as "I want you to see the logic" positions it as a trust-building exercise, not a lecture
- **Options:** Provides two alternative paths (adjusted deliverables, shorter usage rights) so the creator has agency in how they close the gap

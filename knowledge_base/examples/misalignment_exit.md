## Context

Use this example when the deal can't work due to fundamental incompatibility beyond just rate -- for example, the creator requires exclusivity the brand can't offer, the timeline doesn't align, the creator's content style doesn't match brand safety requirements, or the rate gap is so extreme (3x+ over ceiling) that no combination of levers can close it. This is different from a standard walk-away, which is purely about budget.

## Email

Hi {name},

Thank you for the detailed conversation about this campaign. It's been great getting to know more about your work and what you're looking for in brand partnerships.

After reviewing everything on our end, we've realized that {specific_misalignment_reason} doesn't quite align with what this particular campaign requires. This isn't a reflection of your content or your value as a creator -- we genuinely admire what you've built.

We'd love to stay connected and explore opportunities in the future where the fit might be stronger. Your audience and content style are exactly the kind of partnerships we value, and we're confident the right project will come along.

Wishing you all the best, and thanks again for your time.

Best,
{sender_name}

## Key Tactics

- **Honesty:** Names the specific misalignment (exclusivity, timeline, content style) rather than being vague -- this respects the creator's time and intelligence
- **No pressure:** Makes it clear the decision is final for this campaign without leaving ambiguous hope that might string the creator along
- **Relationship preservation:** Separates "this campaign" from "you as a creator" -- the misalignment is situational, not a judgment on their work
- **Specificity:** By naming the actual reason, the creator understands it's a genuine fit issue rather than a polite rejection masking a different concern
